# Research Basis and Non-Claims

CPCF uses results from several fields as formal constraints. A citation supports a definition or
algorithmic distinction; it does not establish that a software collective is a chemical system,
physical phase, causal treatment, or intelligent super-organism.

## Organization, catalysis, and open resources

- Dittrich and Speroni di Fenizio distinguish closed reaction sets from self-maintaining chemical
  organizations: <https://arxiv.org/abs/q-bio/0501016>. CPCF maps this to exact target-bound closure
  and rational nonnegative maintenance balances.
- Huson, Xavier, and Steel give generalized and self-generating RAF distinctions:
  <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38774958/>. CPCF implements multi-catalyst clauses, inhibitors,
  explicit uncatalyzed operations, food provenance, and strictly prior generative layers.
- CatReNet provides exact small-network RAF/CAF algorithm references:
  <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39163485/>. The v0.6 reference kernel now differentially checks
  exact inhibited generalized RAF subsets and strict-prior generative layers for networks within a
  20-transformation bound. It returns unknown beyond the bound and does not claim full CatReNet
  equivalence.
- Horn and Jackson separate stoichiometric structure from dynamical and equilibrium conclusions:
  <https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00251225>. CPCF never promotes a balance identity to an equilibrium,
  stability, or kinetic claim.
- Polettini and Esposito formalize broken conservation relations in open reaction networks:
  <https://doi.org/10.1063/1.4886396>. CPCF uses the open-boundary distinction only as accounting
  guidance.
- Rao and Esposito distinguish internal reactions, chemostatted species, conservation laws, and
  boundary work in open reaction networks:
  <https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevX.6.041064>. CPCF uses only dimensional conservation and boundary
  accounting. It does not compute affinity, entropy production, or thermodynamic efficiency.
- CycleFreeFlux shows why steady mass balance alone does not eliminate thermodynamically infeasible
  cycles: <https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btv096>. CPCF therefore labels its resource
  potential and flow checks structural, never thermodynamic proofs.

## Persistence, concurrency, and verification

- Petri-net siphons provide structural persistence diagnostics, with kinetic assumptions kept
  explicit: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20091352/>,
  <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25986743/>, and
  <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mbs.2007.07.003>. v0.6 exhaustively enumerates minimal siphons within a
  fixed 20-coordinate bound and returns unknown outside it. This is a structural obligation, not a
  persistence theorem under unspecified kinetics.
- Deterministic network calculus supplies backlog and delay semantics when externally attested arrival
  and service curves exist: <https://doi.org/10.1109/18.61109>. v0.6 computes exact finite-horizon
  vertical and horizontal deviations for validated piecewise-rational curves and otherwise retains
  interval utilization or unknown. CPCF does not infer stationarity or use Little's law without its assumptions
  (<https://doi.org/10.1287/opre.9.3.383>).
- Directed hypergraphs model transformations with multiple inputs and outputs:
  <https://doi.org/10.1016/0166-218X(93)90045-P>.
- Constraint-based flux coupling motivates the exact steady-state support and proportionality checks:
  <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC327106/>. CPCF rechecks solver models with rational
  arithmetic and does not infer kinetics.
- Minimal cut sets motivate bounded transformation-removal and enablement enumeration:
  <https://doi.org/10.1093/bioinformatics/btg395>. CPCF reports only the finite declared network,
  not general controllability.
- Finite occurrence prefixes motivate the bounded 1-safe concurrency profile:
  <https://doi.org/10.1016/0167-6423(94)00019-0>. CPCF returns unknown outside unit stoichiometry or
  its event bound and makes no unbounded reachability claim.

## Collective coordination and measurement

- Lorenz et al. show that social influence can reduce diversity without improving collective error:
  <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21576485/>.
- Woolley et al. and Becker et al. motivate separating coordination quality from individual ability
  and from communication density: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20929725/> and
  <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5495222/>. CPCF treats these as empirical design inputs,
  not universal topology laws.
- Doshi and Hauser report that generative AI can improve individual creativity while reducing the
  diversity of collective output: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38996021/>. CPCF therefore treats
  shared artifacts and pre-commit exposure as correlation, not independent support.
- Network topology and diversity effects are task-dependent rather than universally monotone:
  <https://www.nature.com/articles/s44260-024-00025-9> and
  <https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-52837-3>.
- The TARGET statement motivates explicit eligibility, strategies, time zero, outcomes, and analysis
  bindings: <https://www.bmj.com/content/390/bmj-2025-087179.full>. CPCF validates those bindings but
  does not certify causal identification or statistical analysis.

## Planning and software supply chain

- Strong and strong-cyclic FOND policies differ in their treatment of nondeterministic outcomes:
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.09455>. CPCF uses bounded strong planning as its target semantics and
  introduces no fairness or eventual-success assumption. Each branch removes worst-case objects,
  advances its exact resource trajectory, reruns the shared audit kernel, and keeps hypothetical
  output pending until receipt-backed promotion. Signed capability admission remains a separate
  authoritative-loader obligation.
- DSSE authenticates payload type and bytes while leaving key management out of scope:
  <https://github.com/secure-systems-lab/dsse>. CPCF adds pinned identity, policy sequence, trusted
  time, and role-quorum semantics.
- SLSA 1.2 defines build provenance levels: <https://slsa.dev/spec/v1.2/>. CPCF targets Build L2 and
  makes no L3 claim without a verified hardened builder.
