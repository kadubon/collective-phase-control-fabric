# Scientific Boundaries

CPCF borrows executable distinctions from chemical organization theory, RAF systems, open reaction
networks, Petri nets, network calculus, nondeterministic planning, and target-trial design. These are
formal accounting tools, not evidence that software collectives are chemical or physical phases.

The kernel separates:

1. typed provenance and lifecycle;
2. structural hypergraph reachability;
3. strictly prior causal formation;
4. exact closure and maintenance balance;
5. dimensionally consistent finite-resource trajectories;
6. food-supported and target-bound catalytic formation;
7. verifier and independence constraints;
8. reduced-snapshot perturbations; and
9. externally registered empirical evidence.

Mass balance does not establish kinetics, thermodynamics, stability, entropy production, or physical
feasibility. Trial binding does not certify causality or statistical correctness. Structural
planning does not establish general controllability or eventual success.

The [concept-to-code mapping](concept-mapping.md) states which distinctions are executable and which
remain release blockers. Citations and scope limits are in [research basis](research-basis.md).
